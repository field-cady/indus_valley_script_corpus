The "Pashupati" Seal (M-304)

📍 Mohenjo-Daro 🪨 Steatite 🏺 Seal
Sign Sequence (ICIT IDs) +304-405-320-109-808+
𒀀 𒀁 𒀂 𒀃 𒀄

One of the most famous and controversial seals discovered. It depicts a seated figure, often identified as a "proto-Shiva" or "Lord of the Animals" (Pashupati), surrounded by a rhinoceros, water buffalo, elephant, and tiger. The inscription at the top remains undeciphered.

The "Unicorn" Seal

📍 Harappa / Mohenjo-Daro 🪨 Steatite 🏺 Seal Impression
Sign Sequence (Example) +410-017-740-002+

The single-horned "unicorn" bull is by far the most common motif found on Indus Valley seals, appearing on over 60% of them. It is almost always accompanied by a mysterious "standard" or "incense burner" object placed in front of it.

The Dholavira Signboard

📍 Dholavira (Gujarat) 🪨 Gypsum 🪵 Wooden Board (Decayed)
Sign Sequence +740-760-000-742-013/742-552+

Unlike the tiny seals, this was a massive, monumental inscription discovered near the northern gateway of the Dholavira citadel. The symbols were made of white gypsum and likely inlaid into a large wooden board. It is one of the longest single inscriptions discovered, containing ten large signs. The repeated "wheel" sign is highly visible.
